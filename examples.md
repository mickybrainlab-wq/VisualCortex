## PART 1: THE VISUAL BRAIN ARCHITECTURE (V1-V4)

### V1-V2 OPTIMIZED PROMPT (Orientation, Edges & Basic Shapes):

[LINE WORK - V1-V2 FOUNDATION]:
The linework creates instant structure and readability. Three distinct line hierarchies:
1.  **Razor-Sharp Lines:** Used exclusively on the product edges and the CTA button to ensure maximum contrast detection.
2.  **Clean, Precise Lines (Sharp to Moderate):** Used on product photography, text typography, and UI elements for professional readability.
3.  **Soft/Blurred Lines:** Used on lifestyle background elements to create depth without distraction.

[EDGE HIERARCHY - V1-V2 CLEAR PATHWAY]:
- **Level 1 (Extreme):** The Product - pure white product against pure black background OR maximum contrast with the environment. The CTA Button has extreme contrast (bright color against dark background).
- **Level 2 (Medium):** Value Proposition Headline Text, Price Display, Trust Badges (shipping, guarantee, reviews).
- **Level 3 (Low):** Ambient lifestyle elements, gradient backgrounds, subtle textures.

### V3-OPTIMIZED PROMPT (Size, Shape & Dynamic Form):

[PRODUCT SILHOUETTE READABILITY - V3 CRITICAL]:
Create an **ABSOLUTELY UNMISTAKABLE** silhouette of the flagship product. The product must be instantly recognizable even as a pure black shape against a white background. The outer contour is clean, distinctive, and tells the product story without internal details. If someone saw this as a shadow, they would know EXACTLY what product it is within 100ms.

[SIZE HIERARCHY - V3 SIZE DETECTION]:
- **PRIMARY (V3 Dominant):** The Product occupies 35-45% of the frame - large, dominant, and impossible to miss.
- **SECONDARY (V3 Support):** The Headline Text and CTA Button occupy 15-25% of the frame - readable and compelling.
- **TERTIARY (V3 Ambient):** Background elements occupy less than 10% of the frame - atmospheric and non-distracting.

[SHAPE VOCABULARY - V3 PATTERN DETECTION]:
The primary shape language is **Organic Curves + Clean Geometry**. The product features smooth, premium curves (Nature, Softness, Flow) combined with clean geometric UI elements (Stability, Order, Trust). Shape contrast between the organic product and geometric interface creates visual interest.

[DYNAMIC FORM & IMPLIED MOTION - V3 FORM PROCESSING]:
The product is angled dynamically (3/4 view or slightly tilted) suggesting it's interactive and desirable. Leading lines from the product's orientation guide the eye directly to the CTA button. The product appears to "float" or "emerge" from the background, implying presence and value.

### V4-OPTIMIZED PROMPT (Color Processing):

[STRATEGIC COLOR PLACEMENT - V4 ATTENTION]:
V4 processes color. Color is used on THREE elements with a clear hierarchy:
1.  **PRIMARY COLOR (ACCENT):** A vibrant, strategic accent color (#E94E3D for urgency, #00A86B for premium, or #0066FF for trust) used EXCLUSIVELY on the CTA Button. This is the V4 anomaly that demands attention.
2.  **SECONDARY COLOR:** Used on the Product itself (premium product colors - black, silver, white, or brand-specific).
3.  **TERTIARY COLOR:** Used on Background elements (soft, muted, desaturated tones).

[COLOR PALETTE - MAXIMUM 4 COLORS]:
- **PRIMARY COLOR (ACCENT):** [Strategic Color Choice]
    - RED/ORANGE (#E94E3D): Creates urgency, action, and excitement (Best for flash sales, limited offers)
    - GREEN (#00A86B): Creates wealth, premium, and nature associations (Best for organic, luxury, or premium products)
    - BLUE (#0066FF): Creates trust, authority, and security (Best for tech, finance, or high-ticket items)
- **SECONDARY COLOR:** Desaturated Muted Tone - Supports the primary without competing.
- **TERTIARY COLOR:** Pure White or Light Gray - For clean, premium feel.
- **FOURTH COLOR:** Pure Black or Dark Navy - For contrast and depth.

[COLOR PSYCHOLOGY BREAKDOWN]:
- **CTA Accent Color triggers (V4 Emotional Response):** Urgency, action, value, and the Pavlovian "click" response.
- **Product Color triggers (V4 Emotional Response):** Quality, premium value, and desire.
- **Background Color triggers (V4 Emotional Response):** Trust, cleanliness, and professionalism.
- **The combination reinforces:** "This product is premium, this offer is urgent, click now."

## PART 2: THE ONE ATTENTION-GRABBER RULE

[THE ONE ANOMALY - V1-V4 COORDINATION]:
Pick ONE standout element per screen. Everything else follows consistent patterns.

- **THE ANOMALY (The ONLY element that breaks the pattern):** The CALL-TO-ACTION BUTTON.
    - **Contrast (V1-V2):** EXTREME contrast - vibrant color against dark/neutral background.
    - **Shape (V3):** DIFFERENT shape - a solid, large rectangle that contrasts with the organic product shape.
    - **Color (V4):** UNIQUE color - the ONLY saturated element in the entire frame.
    - **Text:** The CTA text is in PURE WHITE against the vibrant button background for maximum readability.
    - **All other elements follow consistent patterns:** Medium contrast, muted colors, similar clean geometry.

---

## PART 3: THE FUSIFORM FACE AREA (FFA) DOMINATION & SOCIAL PROOF

[FACE - SOCIAL PROOF TRUST BUILDER]:
The Fusiform Face Area (FFA) processes faces in milliseconds. Include a **FORWARD-FACING or 3/4 profile** face in a lifestyle context OR a Trust Badge with a human element (customer photo, review avatar). The face triggers instant trust and connection.

[GAZE DIRECTION - THE GUIDING TOOL]:
If using a face, the person's eyes and face are DIRECTED TOWARD the product or CTA button. The gaze is INTENTIONAL and CLEAR, creating a direct leading line from the human connection to the action point.

[FFA OPTIMIZATION - FACE SPECIFICS (if used)]:
- The face has HIGH CONTRAST - bright skin against background.
- The eyes have EXTREME CONTRAST - white sclera, dark pupils.
- The expression is HAPPY, CONFIDENT, or SATISFIED (driving positive association).
- The face is MEDIUM sized (15-20% of frame) - large enough to trigger FFA but not distract from product.

---

## PART 4: CONVERSION OPTIMIZATION ELEMENTS

[TEXT HIERARCHY - CLEAR READABILITY]:
- **H1 HEADLINE (Largest):** 1-2 lines, bold, pure white or extreme contrast, 10-15% of frame.
    - Example: "The Future of [Product Category]"
    - Or: "[Benefit] Without The [Pain Point]"
- **H2 SUBHEADLINE (Medium):** 1-2 lines, clean font, medium contrast, 5-8% of frame.
    - Example: "Revolutionary [Feature] for [Target Audience]"
- **VALUE PROPOSITION (Small):** Bullet points or short phrases, readable but supportive.
    - Examples: "Free Shipping" | "30-Day Guarantee" | "★★★★★ 5,000+ Reviews"

[TRUST SIGNALS - BUILDING AUTHORITY]:
- **Trust Badges:** Located near the CTA button (medium contrast, 5-8% of frame).
    - Shipping Guarantee Icon
    - Money-Back Guarantee Icon
    - Star Ratings / Review Count
    - Security / Payment Badges (Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, SSL)
- **Social Proof:** "Join 10,000+ Happy Customers" or similar social validation text.
- **Limited Availability:** "Only 47 left in stock" or "Sale ends in 02:14:37" (creates urgency).

[PRICE DISPLAY - THE VALUE ANCHOR]:
- **Price (Medium Contrast):** Displayed clearly, larger than supporting text but smaller than the headline.
- **Discount Display (High Contrast if on sale):** "40% OFF" or "Save $200" - uses the same color strategy as the CTA for urgency.
- **Was/Now Price Structure:** Original price crossed out (low contrast, muted color) and new price emphasized (high contrast).

---

## PART 5: THE COMPLETE COMPOSITION

[COMPOSITION RULES]:
- **Rule of Thirds:** The product is on the left or center-left, the CTA button is on the right or bottom-right (following gaze direction and natural reading patterns).
- **Leading Lines:** The product's orientation and any design elements guide the eye in a Z-pattern or F-pattern toward the CTA button.
- **Negative Space:** Generous negative space around the product and CTA isolates these critical elements and prevents distraction.
- **Asymmetry:** Dynamic balance through asymmetrical placement creates visual interest.

[DEPTH LAYERS]:
- **FOREGROUND:** The Product (largest, sharpest, highest contrast).
- **MIDGROUND:** Headline Text, Price Display, CTA Button (medium size, moderate contrast).
- **BACKGROUND:** Lifestyle elements, gradients, subtle textures (smallest, softest, lowest contrast).

[READABILITY TEST - 100ms Scan]:
In the first 0.1 seconds, the viewer sees: "Premium Product + Compelling Headline + Bright CTA Button = Must Click."

[100ms vs 150ms vs 200ms Progression]:
- **0-100ms (V1-V3):** Product shape + Face (if used) + Background recognition.
- **100-150ms (V4):** Color anomaly (the CTA button) grabs attention.
- **150-200ms (FFA + Frontal Lobe):** Text processing begins, comprehension of value proposition and urgency.

- **Below the Fold (Scroll 3):** FAQ, Guarantee, Final CTA.

## PART 7: PSYCHOLOGICAL TRIGGERS

[CONVERSION PSYCHOLOGY - 5 TRIGGERS]:
1.  **Scarcity/Urgency:** "Limited Stock" or "Sale Ends Soon" creates FOMO (Fear of Missing Out).
    - *Visual Activation:* Timer icon, countdown display, or "Only X left" text near CTA.
2.  **Social Proof:** "5,000+ Happy Customers" builds trust through conformity.
    - *Visual Activation:* Star ratings, review count, customer faces/avatars.
3.  **Authority:** Trust badges, secure payment icons, and "As Seen On" logos.
    - *Visual Activation:* Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, BBB, or media logos.
4.  **Reciprocity:** "Free Shipping" or "30-Day Money-Back Guarantee" gives value first.
    - *Visual Activation:* Badges or icons next to the product or CTA.
5.  **Loss Aversion:** "Save $200" is more powerful than "Get $200 off."
    - *Visual Activation:* Crossed-out original price with new price emphasized.

[ACTION DESIRED]:
The viewer should **CLICK THE CTA BUTTON** to purchase, learn more, or add to cart. The composition pushes toward this action through:
- Gaze direction (if face used) pointing to CTA.
- Product orientation leading to CTA.
- Color anomaly (the CTA) being the most visually dominant element.
- Proximity of trust badges to the CTA to reduce anxiety.
